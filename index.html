Larry Wall


(September 27, 1954 - Present)

"Laziness is a programmers main virtue." History

History of Larry Wall:


Larry was born in Los Angeles, California, September 27, 1954. He started higher education at Seattle Pacific Univeristy in 1976. Larry majored in chemistry and music with a hiatus of a couple of years working in Seattle Pacific University's computing center. He would later graduate with a bachelor's degree in Natural and Artificial language. While Larry was in graduate school at the Univeristy of California, Larry and his wife studied linguistics with the goal of finding an unwritten language and creating a writing system for it. They had the intention of using this writing system to translate texts. Due to health reasons, their plans were eventually cancelled. They would remain in USA until Larry complete graduate school. He would then join the NASA Jet Propulsion Labortatory.





Achievements and Contributions


Larry is the author of the rn Usenet client and the popular patch program. Larry has won the International Obfuscated C Code Contest twice and was the recipient of the first Free Software Foundation Award for the Advancement of Free Software in 1998. Larry also developed the Perl interpreter and language and is the co-author of Programming Perl, which is a reosurce for Perl programmers. He would then be employed full-time and develop Perl and write books on Perl. Larry is also known for his sarcastic humour, which he displays in the comments to his source code or on Usenet.
